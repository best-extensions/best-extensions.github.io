2. Scope and Single Purpose

The Extension has a single, clearly defined purpose: to turn the user's PC webcam into a browser-based motion detection and surveillance tool. Its core functions are:

  • Real-time webcam motion detection using pixel-difference analysis.
  • Automatic photo capture when motion is detected (Motion Detection mode) or at user-defined time intervals (Interval Capture mode).
  • A full-screen CCTV-style live monitoring interface with timestamp overlay and motion level indicators.
  • A local photo gallery for reviewing, downloading, and deleting captured images.
  • Automatic saving of captured photos to the user's local Downloads/MotionDetection folder.
  • (PRO only) Email alerts with the captured photo attached, powered by EmailJS.

The Extension does not perform any function unrelated to the above. It does not read, modify, or interact with any web page content (no activeTab or host permission is used beyond what is strictly necessary for the camera monitor tab).

3. Permissions & Their Justification

The Extension requests only the minimum permissions necessary to operate. The following table details each permission, why it is required, and how it is used:

Permission Why it is needed How it is used
storage Required to save user settings persistently across sessions. Stores detection mode, sensitivity level, interval duration, PRO license status, and EmailJS configuration entered by the user. No data is synced to Google servers (uses chrome.storage.local only).
downloads Required to save captured photos to the user's local filesystem. Each captured photo is automatically saved as a JPEG file to the Downloads/MotionDetection/ folder with a date-time filename (e.g., capture_20260331_143000.jpg).
notifications Required to display desktop alerts when motion is detected. Shows a brief Chrome notification informing the user that a photo was captured. No notification data is sent externally.
Camera (getUserMedia) Required to access the webcam feed for motion analysis and photo capture. Camera access is requested only when the user explicitly clicks "Enable Monitoring". The stream is used solely within the Extension's monitor tab for motion detection and capture. It is never transmitted externally. Camera access stops immediately when the user clicks "Stop".

No other permissions are requested or used. The Extension does not request activeTab, tabs, history, cookies, webRequest, clipboardRead, or any host permissions (<all_urls>).

4. Privacy Policy & Data Handling

4.1 Data We Do NOT Collect

OnlineGratis does not collect, process, store, or transmit any of the following:

  • Personal identification information (name, email address, IP address, location).
  • Webcam images, video streams, or any visual data captured by the Extension.
  • Browsing history, visited URLs, or any activity on websites other than the Extension's own monitor page.
  • Device identifiers, hardware fingerprints, or any analytics data.
  • Crash logs or diagnostic data sent to remote servers.

4.2 Data Stored Locally on Your Device

All data generated by the Extension is stored exclusively on your device:

  • Extension settings (detection mode, sensitivity, interval): stored in chrome.storage.local. This data never leaves your browser.
  • Captured photos: saved as JPEG files in your local Downloads/MotionDetection/ folder and temporarily in chrome.storage.local (thumbnail gallery). You can delete them at any time from the built-in gallery or from your file system.
  • EmailJS configuration (Service ID, Template ID, Public Key, recipient address): stored in chrome.storage.local if you choose to configure email alerts. This data is stored only on your device and is used solely to send alerts on your behalf.
  • PRO license code: stored in chrome.storage.local after you enter it. It is validated locally and never transmitted to any verification server by this Extension.

4.3 Data Retention

All locally stored data persists until you:

  • Manually delete photos from the gallery or your Downloads folder.
  • Clear browser storage (via Chrome settings → Privacy and Security → Clear browsing data).
  • Uninstall the Extension (which automatically clears chrome.storage.local data).

4.4 No Remote Data Collection

The Extension does not make any network requests to OnlineGratis servers or any analytics, tracking, or advertising services. The only outbound network activity possible is:

  • Email alert delivery through EmailJS (see Section 7), if and only if you configure and enable it.

5. Camera (Webcam) Access

Access to your device's camera is the core function of this Extension. We take this access seriously and operate under the following strict principles:

  • Explicit activation only: The camera is accessed only when you click "Enable Monitoring". It is never accessed passively, at startup, or in the background.
  • No recording beyond what you initiate: The Extension captures still photos (JPEG) only when motion is detected or at the interval you set. No continuous video recording is stored or transmitted.
  • No streaming to external servers: The webcam feed is processed entirely within your browser tab. No video or image data is sent to any server (unless you have enabled email alerts via EmailJS, in which case the captured JPEG of that specific motion event is included in the email).
  • Immediate stop on user request: Clicking "Stop" or closing the monitor tab immediately releases the camera stream.
  • Browser-level control: You can revoke camera permission at any time via Chrome Settings → Privacy and Security → Site Settings → Camera, or via the permission icon in the address bar of the monitor tab.

Important notice on responsible use: You are solely responsible for using this Extension in compliance with all applicable laws regarding surveillance, recording, and privacy. Do not use this Extension to monitor individuals without their knowledge or consent where such monitoring is prohibited by law. The developer assumes no liability for unlawful use.

6. Local Storage & Downloaded Files

Captured photos are saved automatically to your Downloads/MotionDetection/ folder. File names follow the format capture_YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS.jpg. These files are entirely under your control. The Extension accesses them only to display thumbnails in the built-in gallery.

You can delete photos individually or in bulk from within the gallery, or manually from your file system at any time. The Extension does not send these files to any server unless you have enabled email alerts (see Section 7).

7. Third-Party Service: EmailJS

The PRO version of the Extension optionally integrates with EmailJS (emailjs.com) to send email alerts with a photo attached when motion is detected.

7.1 How it works

  • You must create your own free EmailJS account and obtain a Service ID, Template ID, and Public Key.
  • You enter these credentials into the Extension's popup. They are stored locally on your device only.
  • When motion is detected and email alerts are enabled, the Extension calls the EmailJS API directly from your browser, sending the alert to the email address you specified.
  • EmailJS's free tier allows up to 200 emails per month. A 15-second cooldown between alerts is enforced by the Extension to prevent spam.

7.2 Data shared with EmailJS

When an email alert is sent, the following data is transmitted to EmailJS servers:

  • The captured JPEG photo (embedded as a base64 data URL in the email body).
  • A timestamp of when the capture occurred.
  • The recipient email address you configured.
  • Your EmailJS Service ID, Template ID, and Public Key (used for authentication with your EmailJS account).

This data is handled according to EmailJS's own Privacy Policy and Terms of Service, available at emailjs.com/legal/privacy-policy. OnlineGratis has no control over and no access to data processed by EmailJS.

7.3 This feature is entirely optional

If you do not configure EmailJS, no data is ever sent externally. Email alerts are an opt-in PRO feature. You can disable email alerts at any time by toggling off "Send email on motion detection" in the Extension popup.
